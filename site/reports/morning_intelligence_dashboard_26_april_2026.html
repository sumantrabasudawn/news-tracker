FTA intelligence read

India’s trade outreach is no longer a purely export-market exercise. It is a hedge against tariff volatility, supply-chain fragmentation and geopolitical concentration. The UK, EU and EFTA corridor gives India a developed-market platform; Oman anchors Gulf-linked energy and logistics relevance; New Zealand extends Indo-Pacific diversification; and the US track is the most consequential because it directly intersects with tariff risk, technology access and strategic alignment.

What to track next

The key variable is not the number of FTAs signed, but whether they translate into rules-of-origin flexibility, services mobility, investment commitments, tariff relief, and China+1 supply-chain allocation. For CXO decision-making, the correct question is: which sectors acquire market-access advantage before the next tariff cycle hardens?

3. De-dollarisation tracker

Conclusion

De-dollarisation is occurring at the margin, not at the core. The dollar’s reserve share has drifted down to 56.77%, but the US dollar remains on one side of 89.2% of global FX transactions. Gold purchases by central banks are elevated, which indicates diversification away from pure fiat reserve exposure. But there is still no credible monetary architecture that replaces the dollar in trade finance, liquidity, collateral, derivatives and crisis hedging.

4. What to watch in the next 24–48 hours

Oil, rupee and FPI triangulation

India’s near-term market tone will be decided by whether Brent remains above the $100 threshold, whether the rupee requires visible smoothing, and whether FPI selling continues into the new week. A simultaneous deterioration in all three would move the market from tactical correction to deeper external repricing.

FTA follow-through

Watch whether the India–New Zealand signing narrative is used by policymakers as part of a broader trade-diversification message. The more consequential track remains the US interim framework and whether tariff relief becomes bankable for Indian exporters.

De-dollarisation signal quality

Do not overread bilateral non-dollar settlement headlines. The harder metrics are reserve composition, SWIFT/payment shares, FX turnover, oil invoicing, and central bank gold accumulation. On those measures, fragmentation is visible but dollar displacement remains premature.
